Punctuation
“ Left double quotation mark " ⌥[ 123" &ldquo; In a saner world, Apple would have put the right single and double quotation symbols on the right bracket key, and the left on the left bracket key. But no.
” Right double quotation mark " ⌥⇧[ &rdquo;
‘ Left single quotation mark ' ⌥] 123' &lsquo;
’ Apostrophe/Right single quotation mark ' ⌥⇧] &rsquo;
« Left double angle quotation mark << ⌥\ 123" &laquo; These are quotation marks in French and other languages. Using them as arrows is potentially confusing.
» Right double angle quotation mark >> ⌥⇧\ &raquo;
– En dash - ⌥- 123- &ndash; En dashes are used for ranges, like 0–60, and they’re theoretically the width of an “N”. Em dashes are used to interrupt a thought — they’re traditionally the width of an “M”. The plain old hyphen key is for hyphen-ation.
— Em dash - ⌥⇧- &mdash;
… Ellipsis ... ⌥; 123. &hellip; Semi-colons are also punctuation.
¡ Inverted exclamation mark ⌥1 123! &iexcl; ¡Claro que si!
¿ Inverted question mark ⌥⇧/ 123? &iquest; ¿Por qué no?

Diacritics
é Acute accent ⌥E  E E &eacute;

On Mac OS X: Each of these four types of accents shown can be added to any vowel. Hold Option and press the first key shown. Release Option and then press A, E, I, O or U to type the desired vowel with an accent. (Hold Shift when typing the vowel to capitalize it.) Other than grave, the trigger key is the vowel that most commonly uses that accent (e for é, u for ü and i for î).

On iOS: Simply touch and hold the letter you want to be accented. Tap Shift first if you want it capitalized.
